# Publication Standards

### Sidara Veyn Research Lab

**Author:** Sidara Veyn  
**Role:** Systems Architect — AI-Native Governance & Protocol Design 



## 1. Purpose

This document defines the publication standards governing all formal research outputs produced within the Sidara Veyn Research Lab repository.

The objective is to ensure:

* Consistency
* Traceability
* Citation readiness
* Structural rigor
* Long-term maintainability

These standards apply to all publications regardless of topic, framework, or release date.



## 2. Scope

This standard applies to:

* Research papers
* Governance frameworks
* Governance briefs
* Protocol architecture documents
* Risk analysis reports
* Case studies
* Methodology documents

All repository publications should conform to these requirements unless explicitly designated as exploratory work.



## 3. Publication Objectives

Every publication should contribute toward at least one of the following goals:

* Advancement of governance architecture
* Improvement of protocol design methodologies
* Development of AI-native coordination systems
* Evolution of protocol knowledge systems
* Analysis of governance and systemic risk

Publications should prioritize reusable insights over short-term commentary.

All formal publications must use the following author block:

## 6. Required Sections

Unless a publication category explicitly requires otherwise, documents should contain:

1. Abstract
2. Introduction
3. Problem Statement
4. Framework or System Design
5. Component Analysis
6. Risk and Failure Analysis
7. Implementation Considerations
8. Limitations
9. Conclusion
10. References



## 7. Writing Standards

Publications should be:

* Precise
* Technically rigorous
* Explicit in assumptions
* Structurally organized
* Free of unnecessary marketing language


Preferred style:

* Explanatory
* Analytical
* System-oriented


Avoid:

* Sensational claims
* Unsupported conclusions
* Promotional language
* Excessive speculation



## 8. Terminology Standards

Key terms should be used consistently across all publications.

Examples:

Governance Architecture
Protocol Design System
Knowledge System
Coordination Layer
Composability Risk
Governance Primitive
Governance Lifecycle

If a new term is introduced, it should be explicitly defined.



## 9. Evidence and Reasoning Standards

Publications should clearly distinguish between:

* Observations
* Assumptions
* Models
* Hypotheses
* Conclusions

Reasoning chains should be transparent and reproducible.



## 10. Diagram Standards

Architectural diagrams should:

* Use clear labels
* Show system boundaries
* Identify dependencies
* Indicate information flows where applicable


Diagram assets should be stored within:

/08_framework_assets/



## 11. Failure Analysis Requirement

All major frameworks should include analysis of:

* Structural failure modes
* Incentive failures
* Coordination failures
* Information failures
* Composability risks

Failure analysis is considered a required component of governance architecture research.



## 12. Citation Standards


When citing repository publications, use:

Sidara Veyn — < Publication Title >  
Systems Architect — AI-Native Governance & Protocol Design 


Repository-level references may use:

Sidara Veyn Research Lab (Governance Systems Frameworks)



## 13. Versioning Standards

Publication releases must follow semantic versioning.

v1.0: 
Initial formal release

v1.1: 
Minor clarification or refinement

v1.2–v1.x: 
Incremental improvements

v2.0: 
Structural redesign or major conceptual revision

Version history should be documented when applicable.



## 14. Repository Placement Standards

Publications should be stored according to subject matter.


Examples:

/01_governance_systems/
/02_protocol_knowledge_systems/
/03_defi_risk_frameworks/
/04_ai_coordination_systems/


Cross-domain publications may additionally appear within:

/06_publications/



## 15. Licensing Standards

Unless otherwise specified, publications are released under:

Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0)


Reuse, adaptation, and redistribution are permitted with attribution.



## 16. Review Standard

Before publication, authors should verify:

* Terminology consistency
* Structural completeness
* Version accuracy
* Citation readiness
* Alignment with governance principles
* Alignment with research philosophy



## 17. Publication Quality Threshold

A publication is considered release-ready only when:

* Its objectives are clearly defined
* Its assumptions are explicit
* Its framework is internally consistent
* Its limitations are documented
* Its contribution can be independently understood



## 18. Governing Documents

This standard operates alongside:

* research_philosophy.md
* governance_principles.md
* contributor_model.md

In the event of conflict, governance_principles.md takes precedence.
