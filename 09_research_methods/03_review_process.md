## 1. Purpose

This document defines the structured review process used to evaluate all research outputs within the Sidara Veyn Research Lab.

It applies to:

* governance system designs
* coordination architectures
* protocol knowledge systems
* DeFi risk frameworks
* AI-native governance models

The objective is to ensure consistency, rigor, and structural integrity across all publications.



## 2. Core Thesis

Research review in decentralized systems cannot rely on subjective or informal validation.

Instead:

> review must function as a structured governance system for knowledge production.

Therefore, review is:

* multi-stage
* multi-layered
* system-aware
* reproducible



## 3. Review Architecture

The review process is structured into four sequential layers:

```text id="reviewarch01"
Author Self-Review
        │
Structural Review
        │
Systems Consistency Review
        │
Final Validation Review
```

Each layer serves a distinct validation function.



## 4. Stage I — Author Self-Review

The author performs initial validation before submission.

### Objectives:

* verify structural completeness
* ensure internal consistency
* check methodological alignment
* validate terminology usage

### Key checks:

* correct layer separation
* consistent naming conventions
* absence of logical contradictions
* alignment with research methodology



## 5. Stage II — Structural Review

Focuses on internal architecture of the document.

### Evaluation criteria:

### 5.1 Structural Integrity

* clear section hierarchy
* proper decomposition of concepts
* absence of overlapping categories

### 5.2 Modularity

* independent sections
* reusable conceptual components

### 5.3 Clarity of Representation

* readable system diagrams
* unambiguous definitions
* consistent abstraction levels



## 6. Stage III — Systems Consistency Review

Evaluates alignment with broader research ecosystem.

### Evaluation scope:

* consistency with other publications
* alignment with governance architecture model
* coherence with coordination frameworks
* compatibility with risk and knowledge models

### Key question:

> Does this document integrate coherently into the broader system architecture?



## 7. Stage IV — Final Validation Review

Final quality assurance layer.

### Evaluation criteria:

### 7.1 Conceptual Validity

* correctness of system assumptions
* absence of conceptual drift

### 7.2 Cross-Domain Consistency

* alignment across governance, coordination, and risk models

### 7.3 Temporal Coherence

* consistency with prior and future system states

### 7.4 Reproducibility

* ability for independent reconstruction of reasoning



## 8. Review Dimensions

All reviews evaluate across five dimensions:



### 8.1 Structural Quality

Evaluates architecture of the document.



### 8.2 Conceptual Accuracy

Evaluates correctness of system modeling.



### 8.3 Internal Consistency

Evaluates logical coherence.



### 8.4 System Integration

Evaluates compatibility with broader research ecosystem.



### 8.5 Practical Applicability

Evaluates usefulness for governance systems and protocols.



## 9. Review Workflow

### Step 1 — Pre-Review Check

Author verifies internal consistency.

### Step 2 — Structural Analysis

Document structure is evaluated.

### Step 3 — System Mapping

Document is mapped to research ecosystem.

### Step 4 — Consistency Validation

Cross-document consistency is checked.

### Step 5 — Final Approval Decision

Document is accepted, revised, or rejected.



## 10. AI-Augmented Review Support

AI systems assist review by:

### 10.1 Structural Parsing

* detecting inconsistencies
* mapping document structure

### 10.2 Cross-Document Comparison

* identifying conflicts across publications
* detecting duplication or drift

### 10.3 Terminology Consistency

* enforcing standardized definitions
* detecting semantic drift

### 10.4 Risk Detection

* identifying governance or systemic inconsistencies

AI supports review but does not replace architectural judgment.



## 11. Review Classification Outcomes

Each document is classified as:



### 11.1 Approved

Meets all structural and conceptual requirements.



### 11.2 Revision Required

Requires targeted improvements.



### 11.3 Major Rework

Fundamental structural or conceptual issues present.



### 11.4 Rejected

Not aligned with research methodology or system architecture.



## 12. Common Review Failure Modes



### 12.1 Structural Incoherence

Poorly defined system layers.



### 12.2 Conceptual Drift

Inconsistent or evolving definitions.



### 12.3 Cross-System Misalignment

Conflicts with other research outputs.



### 12.4 Over-Abstraction

Loss of practical system grounding.



### 12.5 Under-Specification

Insufficient detail for reproducibility.



## 13. Governance Role of Review Process

The review system functions as:

* quality control mechanism
* knowledge governance layer
* structural integrity validator
* system evolution filter

It ensures that research outputs remain consistent with the overall architecture of the research lab.



## 14. Application Scope

This review process applies to:

* all governance system papers
* AI coordination frameworks
* protocol design documents
* DeFi risk analyses
* knowledge graph systems
* case studies and briefs



## Conclusion

A rigorous review process is essential for maintaining structural integrity in complex research systems.

By formalizing review as a multi-layer governance mechanism, this framework ensures that all outputs are consistent, reproducible, and aligned with the broader architectural vision of AI-native governance systems.
